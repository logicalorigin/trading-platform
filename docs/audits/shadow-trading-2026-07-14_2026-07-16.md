# Shadow trading audit: July 14–16, 2026

Audit cutoff: July 16, 2026 after the US options close. Times are US Eastern unless stated otherwise.

## Scope and evidence standard

This audit covers the Pyrus Signals Options shadow deployment `7e2e4e6f-749f-4e65-a011-87d3559a23b0`. It does not represent live-broker fills; no live broker orders or fills were found in scope.

Observed facts come from persisted execution events, shadow orders/fills/positions, runtime recorder evidence, and Massive consolidated option quotes/trades. The primary counterfactual replays each recorded entry profile with executable-bid peaks and 10-second double-last/double-ask confirmation for regular hard stops and runner trails. It suppresses only `overnight_risk_exit`; early invalidation, profitable-runner management, and expiry liquidation remain enabled.

Source and runtime now agree on the approved doctrine. `overnightMinGainExitEnabled` defaults to `false`, so the blanket final-15-minute minimum-gain exit is disabled without disabling the profitable-runner overnight stop. Regular hard stops and runner trails require an elected double-last or double-ask confirmation; a quote midpoint cannot authorize them. The API was rebuilt twice in place under the same supervisor PID after the source change and ledger correction, and `/api/healthz` returned 200 after each reload.

Counterfactual P&L uses the first displayed bid capable of filling the whole recorded position and subtracts the ledger's recorded exit fee. It is a conservative shadow estimate, not a broker execution guarantee.

- This is not a recommendation to hold everything. Six replacements are confirmed hard stops, four are confirmed runner trails, and one is early invalidation. The five July 16 positions are retained consistently at the cutoff, but their July 17 outcomes remain unknown in this audit.

## Applied doctrine and ledger rewrite

The evidence-supported rule set is now implemented:

1. `overnightMinGainExitEnabled` is explicit and defaults to `false`.
2. Only the blanket `+10%` final-15-minute floor is disabled.
3. Hard stops and regular runner trails require executable double-last or double-ask confirmation. Immediate midpoint hard stops are not permitted.
4. Early invalidation and the tighter profitable-runner overnight trail remain enabled.
5. Maintenance remains catastrophe-only. DRAM is corrected to the approved first valid dynamic runner election, and T uses the approved earliest double-last election.
6. Independent expiration liquidation remains enabled; expiry-day positions can stay managed until that safety path.
7. The historical rewrite applies the chosen rules consistently to every in-scope lifecycle. Winners and losers are not cherry-picked.
