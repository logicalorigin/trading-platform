## Lingering ELU/CPU and Database-Line Audit — 2026-07-12

- A three-minute direct watch and a separate 100-second low-probe control both reproduced sustained pressure after retained bars were compact and PostgreSQL physical connects were gated. In the direct watch, ELU averaged about 0.83 (10/13 persisted samples above 0.80), loop p95 averaged 189 ms and reached 334 ms, and API request p95 averaged about 1.98 s. The low-probe control still measured roughly 60–72% of one CPU core, ruling out the heavy runtime endpoint as the primary driver.
- Heap cycled and reclaimed between roughly 1.2 and 1.85 GB while RSS stayed near 2.5 GB. This is allocation/GC churn, not an observed monotonic retained-memory leak. A 48-second inspector profile attributed about 19% of busy time to GC; PostgreSQL row decoding, Drizzle mapping, signal-state shaping, SSE serialization, market-session resolution, and shadow-risk hashing were also material.
- The largest closed-market avoidable driver was Massive option polling: 3,303 REST requests in 130.5 seconds (25.3/s) across only 36 contracts. Thirty-two contracts were scanner-retained with no subscriber consuming those retained quotes; only four were open-position demand. The scanner's retained five-minute quote leases are unnecessary after each snapshot pass. Open-position risk is needed in-session, but its two-second unchanged off-hours polling and per-tick metadata persistence are not.
- The legacy Signal Monitor evaluator contradicts its startup comment saying the scan was removed and must not be re-added. The watched API had `PYRUS_SIGNAL_MONITOR_BAR_EVALUATION_ENABLED=1`, so the worker was active: up to 48 symbols and 1,000 bars per symbol per rotation step, with unchanged no-signal cells eligible for repeated evaluation. Its evaluated-key set is unbounded for remembered signal cells, and the enabled behavior lacks a focused test. This path is not needed in the normal stream-produced runtime.
- The server-side Signal Monitor producer is needed, but its forming-quote trigger is not functioning efficiently. Forming updates reach the matrix every second, while the evaluator explicitly loads completed bars and filters out the provisional minute. Thus repeated intra-minute work cannot change the completed series. Bar-close/correction triggers should drive server evaluation; forming ticks should remain only for visible UI freshness.
- Cold stored-bar hydration remains an open restart risk. Never-warmed cells bypass the nominal 64-cell cap and can request roughly 1.44–5.76 million rows for a 500–2,000-symbol universe. Cold candidates include the 1-minute frame even though source comments say it needs no backfill. A singleton in-flight flag also lets the first profile suppress concurrent distinct-profile refreshes. Cross-cycle delta reuse was observed working in steady state, but it does not remove this cold-start risk.
- The diagnostics collector is needed, but the 15-second full snapshot is overbuilt: about 577 KB per full runtime object, including about 383 KB of 2,000 mostly empty stock per-symbol rows and 113 KB of admission detail. It also rereads the growing incidents JSONL and `/proc`, writes 12 diagnostic snapshot rows per tick, and separately serializes for four diagnostic SSE clients. A light pressure heartbeat and slower/on-demand detail snapshot should be separated.
- Matrix SSE is absent from generic SSE stream/open-close counters and bypasses the generic serializer, even though its direct `writeEvent` was the most expensive stream serialization path in the profile. Its five-second status timer constructs all 2,000 stock per-symbol diagnostic objects to extract a small summary. Account and marketing streams also use per-subscriber timers; account prewarms an inactive-account live stream. Algo Cockpit sharing/no-overlap is functioning, but its five-second poll combines hot state with slower performance/profile work.
- The full-universe stock WebSocket is needed during live sessions. Its 60-second stale watchdog is not market-session aware, so an expected-quiet Sunday socket repeatedly reconnects, authenticates, and resubscribes about 2,000 symbols / 4,000 channel parameters.
- PostgreSQL physical-connect gating, idle-client reuse, compact-bar storage, and the separate trading pool were observed functioning. There were no original-window SQLSTATE 53300 errors and no sustained raw pool queue. Transient saturation still reached all 12 shared slots plus an admission queue of eight; 55 slow queries overlapped for 159.6 seconds and 11 acquisitions took 653–1,343 ms.
